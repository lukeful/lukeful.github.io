FRC Robot Object Detection & Trajectory Generation

During the FRC Crescendo Season, orange, hollow discs on the ground called "notes" were used to score points. In order to improve intake of the robot during autonomous period (no driver/operator) allowed, object detection and trajectory generation were used in conjunction to correctly intake game pieces. Rather than relying on computationally heavy computer vision technology, I developed a light weight model that uses HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) Color Filtering. However, the USB Camera had a very limited FOV and could not see notes in close vicinity. A-FIB Research and A-FIB Detector App

I was the primary Python programmer who coded 14 machine learning classifiers using 18 predictors for detection of atrial fibrillation (A-FIB), a heart condition that causes irregular heartbeats and affects nearly 500,000 Americans. Through ML, I achieved accuracies above 95%. There was extensive investigation into the most accurate classifiers with respect to various features/estimators. The incorporation of a previously financial estimator, volatility, was also explored.

Later on, I developed a local web app to combine a machine learning classifier with heartrate data from a smartwatch, such as an Apple Watch, to determine if there were preliminary signs of atrial fibrillation for free. Currently, the local web app's backend (Python/ML processing) has not been added to the model.
